# Shreeji Hotels — Portfolio Performance Report
## Comparative Market Analysis: 2024 vs 2025

---

## Executive Summary

Across 2024–2025, the London hospitality market experienced sustained margin compression, declining ADR, and structural cost escalation driven by National Living Wage increases, employer NIC changes, and reduced business rate relief. Against this backdrop, the Shreeji portfolio of 10 London hotels delivered a fundamentally different performance profile to the broader independent hotel market.

This report benchmarks Shreeji's portfolio against 15 comparable independent hotels tracked in the Market Pulse platform, covering the same geography and time period. The findings are clear:

- **Occupancy**: Shreeji operates at near-full capacity (~95%) — more than 20 percentage points above the market average (~75%)
- **Revenue Per Available Room**: Shreeji's RevPAR of £120 materially exceeds the market comp set average of £95–102
- **ADR Resilience**: 87% of comparable market hotels experienced ADR declines; Shreeji's portfolio-weighted ADR grew +3.6%
- **Portfolio Revenue**: Shreeji's combined revenue held stable at +0.5% YoY — outperforming London-wide RevPAR which declined -0.4% (PwC/STR, YTD Nov 2025)

## 6. Conclusion

In a market characterised by:
- Declining ADR across the board
- GOPPAR falling 8.3% (Knight Frank)
- Profit margins contracting 3 percentage points
- 95% of operators reporting wage cost increases
- £3.4 billion in additional sector-wide operating costs
- New supply adding 5,300+ rooms

...the Shreeji portfolio delivered:
- **Near-full occupancy** (~94–97%), more than 12–23 percentage points above the market
- **Positive portfolio-weighted ADR growth** (+3.6%) against a market declining -0.2% to -6.9%
- **Stable total revenue** (+0.5%) outperforming London's overall RevPAR trajectory
- **RevPAR of £120** — a consistent £18–25 premium over the comparable market

These results reflect a portfolio that is not merely surviving the current headwinds but operating at a fundamentally higher performance tier than the broader London independent hotel market. The combination of occupancy dominance and rate resilience in a period of structural market stress represents a compelling demonstration of operational strength and demand capture capability.
